<compilation_rules>
- use the analyst memo text as the source structure; do not invent new nodes or relations outside it
- the memo may contain both explicit support and analyst inference; explicit support is preferred
- inference-only claims may be used only when they create a more defensible company-level structure and the uncertainty field does not signal material ambiguity
- if uncertainty suggests the relation may be wrong or overly neat, omit it
- preserve family-level offerings that appear in the memo as real offering nodes; do not flatten parent-child offering structure into only leaf offerings
- preserve split-franchise hierarchy when the memo supports it; if a franchise is modeled as a family plus differentiated children, keep that structure in the graph
- preserve distinct named franchises as standalone offerings when the memo frames them as standalone platforms or businesses, even if the memo also mentions their cloud or services lines inside a broader horizontal parent
- do not use Company -> OFFERS unless the memo clearly requires it as a true fallback
- compile these relations when support is strong enough:
  - Company -> HAS_SEGMENT -> each segment
  - BusinessSegment -> OFFERS -> each first-order offering anchored to that segment and represented as its own full `[Offering]` block
  - Offering -> OFFERS -> child offering when `Parent Offering` or `Child Offerings` makes the hierarchy supportable
  - BusinessSegment -> SERVES -> segment customer_types
  - BusinessSegment -> SELLS_THROUGH -> segment channels
  - Offering -> SELLS_THROUGH -> offering-specific channels
  - Offering -> MONETIZES_VIA -> offering revenue_models
  - Company -> OPERATES_IN -> corporate_scope operating_geographies
  - Company -> PARTNERS_WITH -> corporate_scope key_partners
- if a parent family and its differentiated children are all supportable, preserve the parent node even when the children carry more specific monetization or channel detail
- do not materialize a parent offering node that appears only as a reference inside `Parent Offering` unless that parent also has its own full `[Offering]` block
- if a candidate child is only a broad descriptive bucket, accounting rollup, or unnamed generic category rather than a stable named offering family from the memo, omit it
- if an offering has a parent_offering and only appears under that parent, do not also create a direct segment -> offering edge unless the memo independently supports that direct anchor
- prefer omission over weak guesses
</compilation_rules>
