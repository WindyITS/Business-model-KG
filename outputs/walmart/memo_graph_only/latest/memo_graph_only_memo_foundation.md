# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Walmart operates a global omnichannel retail ecosystem organized into three reportable segments: Walmart U.S., Walmart International, and Sam's Club U.S. The business model centers on high-volume, low-margin merchandise sales executed through an everyday low price (EDLP) strategy, augmented by high-margin recurring revenue streams including membership subscriptions (Walmart+, Sam's Club tiers), advertising solutions, marketplace services, and financial services. The company integrates physical retail footprints (supercenters, neighborhood markets, warehouse clubs) with digital commerce platforms, leveraging stores as fulfillment nodes for eCommerce operations. International operations include distinct digital assets Flipkart (eCommerce) and PhonePe (digital payments) in India.

## Explicit Support:
- "We are a people-led, technology-powered omnichannel retailer"
- "Our operations comprise three reportable segments: Walmart U.S., Walmart International and Sam's Club U.S."
- "Our strategy is to make every day easier for busy families... including our commitment to price leadership"
- "These offerings represent mutually reinforcing pieces of our omnichannel model... membership, advertising, marketplace and fulfillment services, and financial services"

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Walmart U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Mass merchandiser operating the company's largest segment (68% of fiscal 2026 net sales), combining supercenters, neighborhood markets, and digital commerce (walmart.com) into an integrated omnichannel retail operation focused on grocery, general merchandise, and health/wellness categories.

### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart U.S. is our largest segment and operates 4,611 stores in the U.S."
- "Walmart U.S. had net sales of $483.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 68% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"
- "Walmart U.S. is a mass merchandiser of consumer products, operating under the 'Walmart' and 'Walmart Neighborhood Market' brands, as well as walmart.com"

### Customer Types:
- consumers | Primary customer base described as "busy families" and individual shoppers purchasing for personal/household use across grocery and general merchandise categories
- small businesses | Marketplace sellers utilizing supply chain and fulfillment capabilities, and recipients of advertising solutions

### Channels:
- retail | Physical stores including supercenters and neighborhood markets serving as primary fulfillment points
- online | walmart.com and Walmart mobile application for eCommerce transactions and digital services

## [Segment] Walmart International
### Role in Business Model:
Multi-format retail operations across 18 countries outside the U.S., operating supercenters, supermarkets, warehouse clubs (including Sam's Club format), and cash-and-carry formats, with significant digital presence including majority-owned subsidiaries Flipkart and PhonePe in India. Represents the company's primary vehicle for international growth and geographic diversification.

### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart International is our second largest segment and operates 5,743 stores across 18 countries outside of the U.S."
- "Walmart International operates through our wholly-owned subsidiaries in Canada, Chile, China, and Africa... and our majority-owned subsidiaries in India"
- "Walmart International had net sales of $130.4 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 19% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"

### Customer Types:
- consumers | International shoppers across diverse markets seeking everyday low prices on groceries and general merchandise
- small businesses | Cash-and-carry customers and marketplace participants in international markets

### Channels:
- retail | International physical store formats including supercenters, supermarkets, and warehouse clubs
- online | Country-specific eCommerce websites (walmart.com.mx, walmart.ca, samclub.cn) and digital platforms (Flipkart, PhonePe)

## [Segment] Sam's Club U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Membership-only warehouse club requiring paid subscription for access, generating significant operating income from membership fees alongside merchandise sales. Operates 601 clubs in the U.S. and Puerto Rico with integrated eCommerce capabilities and distinct tiered membership structure.

### Explicit Support:
- "Sam's Club U.S. is a membership-only warehouse club that operates 601 clubs in 44 states in the U.S. and Puerto Rico"
- "As a membership-only club, membership income is a significant component of the segment's operating income"
- "Sam's Club U.S. had net sales of $93.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 13% of our consolidated fiscal 2026 net sales"

### Customer Types:
- small businesses | Primary target for warehouse club model, purchasing in bulk for commercial operations
- consumers | Households and individual members seeking value through bulk purchasing and member benefits

### Channels:
- retail | Physical warehouse club locations with curbside pickup and in-club shopping
- online | samsclub.com and Sam's Club mobile application for digital commerce and delivery services
