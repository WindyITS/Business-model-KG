# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Walmart Inc. operates a global omnichannel retail ecosystem centered on three reportable segments: Walmart U.S., Walmart International, and Sam's Club U.S. The business model generates revenue through retail merchandise sales, membership subscriptions (Walmart+, Sam's Club), advertising platforms, marketplace services, and financial products. The company leverages physical stores and digital platforms (eCommerce websites and mobile applications) to serve consumers and small businesses across 19 countries, supported by proprietary private brands and third-party seller marketplaces.

## Explicit Support:
- "Walmart Inc. ... is a people-led, technology-powered omnichannel retailer"
- "Our operations comprise three reportable segments: Walmart U.S., Walmart International and Sam's Club U.S."
- "we continue to heavily invest in omnichannel and eCommerce innovation"
- "membership, advertising, marketplace and fulfillment services, and financial services"

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Walmart U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Largest segment operating mass merchandising formats including supercenters and Neighborhood Markets, integrated with eCommerce through walmart.com and mobile applications. Serves as the anchor for omnichannel innovation and membership programs (Walmart+).
### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart U.S. is our largest segment... operates 4,611 stores in the U.S."
- "Walmart U.S. is a mass merchandiser of consumer products"
- "Walmart U.S. had net sales of $483.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 68% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | primary shopper base for mass merchandise and grocery
- small businesses | marketplace sellers and advertising clients
### Channels:
- retail | 4,611 physical stores including supercenters and Neighborhood Markets
- online | walmart.com and Walmart mobile application

## [Segment] Walmart International
### Role in Business Model:
Second largest segment operating diverse retail and wholesale formats (supercenters, supermarkets, warehouse clubs, cash and carry) across 18 countries outside the U.S., including majority-owned digital platforms Flipkart and PhonePe in India. Adapts merchandising to local markets while leveraging global ecosystem capabilities.
### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart International is our second largest segment and operates 5,743 stores across 18 countries"
- "operates through our wholly-owned subsidiaries... and our majority-owned subsidiaries in India"
- "Walmart International had net sales of $130.4 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 19% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | primary shopper base across international markets
- small businesses | cash and carry customers and marketplace sellers
### Channels:
- retail | physical stores (supercenters, supermarkets, warehouse clubs, cash and carry)
- online | country-specific websites and mobile applications including flipkart.com and PhonePe

## [Segment] Sam's Club U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Membership-only warehouse club segment generating significant income from membership fees. Provides bulk merchandise and services to members through physical clubs and eCommerce, with tiered membership structure (Club and Plus).
### Explicit Support:
- "Sam's Club U.S. is a membership-only warehouse club... operates 601 clubs"
- "membership income is a significant component of the segment's operating income"
- "Sam's Club U.S. had net sales of $93.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 13% of our consolidated fiscal 2026 net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | individual members purchasing for personal/household use
- small businesses | members purchasing for business operations (implied by warehouse club model and bulk offerings)
### Channels:
- retail | 601 physical clubs
- online | samsclub.com and Sam's Club mobile application

# OFFERINGS
## [Offering] Walmart+ Membership Program
### Role in Business Model:
Subscription membership providing enhanced omnichannel shopping benefits including unlimited free shipping, delivery from store, fuel discounts, and mobile Scan & Go. Drives customer loyalty and recurring revenue within the Walmart U.S. ecosystem.
### Explicit Support:
- "Our Walmart+ membership offering provides enhanced omnichannel shopping benefits including unlimited free shipping on eligible items with no order minimum, unlimited delivery from store, fuel discounts, mobile Scan & Go and access to additional member benefits"
### Segment Anchors:
- Walmart U.S. | described as part of Walmart U.S. omnichannel strategy
### Parent Offering:
- none
### Child Offerings:
- none
### Channels:
- online | subscription acquired and managed through digital platforms
### Revenue Models:
- subscription | recurring membership fees

## [Offering] Sam's Club Membership Program
### Role in Business Model:
Tiered membership program required for access to Sam's Club locations and services. Generates significant operating income through annual fees and creates member lock-in for bulk purchasing and ancillary services.
### Explicit Support:
- "The following two membership tiers are available: Club membership for a $50 annual fee and Plus membership for a $110 annual fee"
- "membership income is a significant component of the segment's operating income"
### Segment Anchors:
- Sam's Club U.S. | core offering of the segment, required for access
### Parent Offering:
- none
### Child Offerings:
- Club Membership Tier | entry-level tier at $50 annual fee providing basic club access and curbside pickup
- Plus Membership Tier | premium tier at $110 annual fee providing free delivery-from-club, free shipping on orders $50+, and early shopping hours
### Channels:
- none
### Revenue Models:
- subscription | annual membership fees
