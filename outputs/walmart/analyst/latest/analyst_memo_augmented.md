# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Walmart Inc. operates a global omnichannel retail ecosystem centered on three reportable segments—Walmart U.S., Walmart International, and Sam's Club U.S.—that integrate physical retail formats (supercenters, neighborhood markets, warehouse clubs) with digital platforms (eCommerce websites, mobile applications) and a growing portfolio of high-margin service businesses. The business model generates revenue through retail merchandise sales (powered by proprietary private brands and third-party marketplaces), membership subscriptions (Walmart+, Sam's Club tiers), advertising platforms, fulfillment and data services for sellers, and consumer financial products. The company leverages an EDLP/EDLC operating philosophy, a global supply chain infrastructure, and AI-powered technologies to serve consumers and small businesses across 19 countries, with distinct but mutually reinforcing digital platforms (Flipkart, PhonePe) anchoring the India market.

## Explicit Support:
- "Walmart Inc. ... is a people-led, technology-powered omnichannel retailer"
- "Our operations comprise three reportable segments: Walmart U.S., Walmart International and Sam's Club U.S."
- "we continue to heavily invest in omnichannel and eCommerce innovation"
- "membership, advertising, marketplace and fulfillment services, and financial services"
- "EDLP is our pricing philosophy under which we price items at a low price every day"
- "EDLC is our commitment to control expenses so our cost savings can be passed along to our customers"

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Walmart U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Largest segment operating mass merchandising formats including supercenters, discount stores, and Neighborhood Markets, integrated with eCommerce through walmart.com and mobile applications. Serves as the anchor for omnichannel innovation, membership programs (Walmart+), and the development of high-margin service offerings (advertising, marketplace, fulfillment, financial services) that leverage the physical store footprint for pickup, delivery, and last-mile logistics.
### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart U.S. is our largest segment... operates 4,611 stores in the U.S."
- "Walmart U.S. is a mass merchandiser of consumer products, operating under the 'Walmart' and 'Walmart Neighborhood Market' brands"
- "Walmart U.S. had net sales of $483.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 68% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | primary shopper base for mass merchandise and grocery
- small businesses | marketplace sellers, advertising clients, and recipients of data analytics services
### Channels:
- retail | 4,611 physical stores including supercenters and Neighborhood Markets
- online | walmart.com and Walmart mobile application

## [Segment] Walmart International
### Role in Business Model:
Second largest segment operating diverse retail and wholesale formats (supercenters, supermarkets, warehouse clubs, cash and carry) across 18 countries outside the U.S., including wholly-owned operations in Canada, Chile, China, and Africa, and majority-owned digital platforms Flipkart and PhonePe in India. Adapts merchandising to local markets while leveraging global ecosystem capabilities including shared private brands, advertising technology, and fulfillment infrastructure.
### Explicit Support:
- "Walmart International is our second largest segment and operates 5,743 stores across 18 countries"
- "operates through our wholly-owned subsidiaries... and our majority-owned subsidiaries in India"
- "Walmart International had net sales of $130.4 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 19% of our fiscal 2026 consolidated net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | primary shopper base across international markets
- small businesses | cash and carry customers, marketplace sellers, and advertising clients
### Channels:
- retail | physical stores (supercenters, supermarkets, warehouse clubs, cash and carry)
- online | country-specific websites and mobile applications including flipkart.com, walmart.ca, walmart.com.mx, and PhonePe

## [Segment] Sam's Club U.S.
### Role in Business Model:
Membership-only warehouse club segment generating significant income from membership fees through a tiered structure (Club and Plus). Provides bulk merchandise and ancillary services to members through 601 physical clubs and eCommerce, with a proprietary private label (Member's Mark) and loyalty rewards program (Sam's Cash) reinforcing member retention and lifetime value.
### Explicit Support:
- "Sam's Club U.S. is a membership-only warehouse club... operates 601 clubs"
- "membership income is a significant component of the segment's operating income"
- "Sam's Club U.S. had net sales of $93.0 billion for fiscal 2026, representing 13% of our consolidated fiscal 2026 net sales"
### Customer Types:
- consumers | individual members purchasing for personal/household use
- small businesses | members purchasing for business operations and resale
### Channels:
- retail | 601 physical clubs
- online | samsclub.com and Sam's Club mobile application
