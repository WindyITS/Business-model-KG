# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Palantir operates a dual-segment enterprise software business targeting complex data integration challenges that government agencies and large commercial institutions cannot solve internally. The company monetizes through four vertically integrated software platforms—Gotham, Foundry, Apollo, and AIP—delivered via direct enterprise sales and cloud partnerships, generating revenue through software licensing and implementation services.

## Explicit Support:
- "We have built four principal software platforms, Palantir Gotham (“Gotham”), Palantir Foundry (“Foundry”), Palantir Apollo (“Apollo”), and Palantir Artificial Intelligence Platform (“AIP”)"
- "Of the $2.9 billion in revenue that we generated in 2024, 55% came from customers in the government segment, and 45% came from customers in the commercial segment"
- "Our customer acquisition strategy generally targets large-scale, hard-to-execute opportunities at large government and commercial institutions"

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Government
### Role in Business Model:
Primary revenue generator (55% of 2024 revenue) serving defense agencies, the intelligence community, and civilian government functions with secure data integration, operational analytics, and AI platforms. Represents the historical foundation of the company and remains the core anchor for the Gotham platform.
### Explicit Support:
- "Of the $2.9 billion in revenue that we generated in 2024, 55% came from customers in the government segment"
- "We were founded in 2003 and started building software for the intelligence community in the United States"
- "Gotham is now used broadly across government functions"
### Customer Types:
- Government agencies | Primary constituency including defense agencies, intelligence community, public health administrators, and military organizations
### Channels:
- Direct sales | Account-based sales force targeting specific government contracts and large-scale implementations
- System integrators | Partnerships with government contractors and integrators for complex deployments

## [Segment] Commercial
### Role in Business Model:
Growth segment (45% of 2024 revenue) targeting large enterprises across approximately 90 industries with data operating systems and AI platforms. Focused on becoming the "central operating system" for individual institutions and entire industries through scalable platform adoption.
### Explicit Support:
- "45% came from customers in the commercial segment"
- "All of our commercial customers now use it [Foundry]"
- "software is currently used across approximately 90 industries"
### Customer Types:
- Large enterprises | Explicitly targeted as "large-scale, hard-to-execute opportunities at large... commercial institutions" with complex data environments
- Developers | Targeted via Developer Tier offering for exploration and innovation without enterprise procurement barriers
### Channels:
- Direct sales | Account-based sales force for enterprise acquisition
- Cloud partnerships | Partnerships with public, private, and hybrid cloud providers to leverage existing enterprise relationships and computing infrastructure
- Resellers | Channel sales relationships with third-party providers and technology vendors
- Online | Digital self-service access for Developer Tier

# OFFERINGS
## [Offering] Palantir Gotham
### Role in Business Model:
Data integration and analytics platform designed for government operations, enabling pattern identification across intelligence datasets and facilitating hand-offs between analysts and operational users for real-world response coordination.
### Explicit Support:
- "Gotham enables users to identify patterns hidden deep within datasets... It also facilitates the hand-off between analysts and operational users"
- "Gotham is now used broadly across government functions"
### Segment Anchors:
- Government | Primary platform for defense and intelligence operations, though applicable to broader government functions
### Parent Offering:
- none | First-order platform distinct from other offerings
### Child Offerings:
- none | No named child variants or tiers described
### Channels:
- none | Distributed through segment-level direct sales and system integrators
### Revenue Models:
- Licensing | Software platform rights for government deployment and usage
- Service fees | Implementation, integration, and strategic deployment services for complex installations

## [Offering] Palantir Foundry
### Role in Business Model:
Commercial-focused data operating system that transforms organizational operations by creating a centralized platform for data integration, pipeline management, and cross-functional analytics. Serves as the primary commercial platform and foundation for industry-specific operating systems.
### Explicit Support:
- "Foundry transforms the ways organizations operate by creating a central operating system for their data"
- "All of our commercial customers now use it"
### Segment Anchors:
- Commercial | Primary platform for commercial data operations and enterprise transformation
### Parent Offering:
- none | First-order platform distinct from other offerings
### Child Offerings:
- Developer Tier | Limited access tier providing restricted Foundry capabilities for developer exploration
### Channels:
- none | Distributed through segment-level direct sales and cloud partnerships
### Revenue Models:
- Licensing | Enterprise software platform licensing for commercial deployment
- Service fees | Professional services and implementation for enterprise data integration projects

## [Offering] Palantir AIP
### Role in Business Model:
Artificial Intelligence Platform that operationalizes generative AI and large language models within existing Palantir platforms. Functions as a horizontal AI capability layer across both government and commercial segments, enabling responsible AI deployment with security and audit controls.
### Explicit Support:
- "AIP leverages the power of our existing machine learning technologies alongside generative AI models... directly within Gotham and/or Foundry"
- "AIP is seamlessly bundled with existing Palantir offerings such as the Foundry, Gotham, and Apollo platforms"
### Segment Anchors:
- Government | Deployed alongside Gotham for government AI use cases and operational decision-making
- Commercial | Deployed alongside Foundry for commercial enterprise AI applications
### Parent Offering:
- none | First-order platform distinct from but integrated with Gotham and Foundry
### Child Offerings:
- Developer Tier | Limited access tier providing restricted AIP capabilities for developer exploration and innovation
### Channels:
- none | Distributed through platform bundling via segment-level direct sales
### Revenue Models:
- Licensing | AI platform capabilities licensed as part of bundled solutions or standalone
- Service fees | Implementation services for AI workflow integration and responsible AI deployment

## [Offering] Palantir Apollo
### Role in Business Model:
Cloud-agnostic infrastructure and continuous delivery platform enabling deployment of Palantir software and customer-owned software across any environment (cloud, on-premises, or rugged). Serves as the operational backbone for continuous updates and multi-environment deployment.
### Explicit Support:
- "Apollo is a cloud-agnostic, single control layer that coordinates ongoing delivery of new features, security updates, and platform configurations"
- "began offering as a commercial solution in 2021"
### Segment Anchors:
- Government | Enables secure Gotham deployment in classified and defense environments
- Commercial | Enables Foundry deployment and offered as standalone commercial infrastructure solution
### Parent Offering:
- none | First-order infrastructure platform distinct from application platforms
### Child Offerings:
- none | No named child variants described
### Channels:
- none | Distributed through segment-level direct sales and cloud partnerships
### Revenue Models:
- Licensing | Infrastructure control layer and deployment management licensing
- Service fees | Configuration management and continuous delivery services

## [Offering] Developer Tier
### Role in Business Model:
Limited access tier designed to lower barriers to entry for developers and small-scale exploration, allowing innovation on Foundry and AIP without significant upfront enterprise costs. Represents a bottom-up adoption strategy distinct from the enterprise sales motion.
### Explicit Support:
- "in 2024, we introduced Developer Tier, offering limited access to Foundry and AIP in the United States and select countries"
- "allows developers to explore, innovate, and develop without significant upfront enterprise costs"
### Segment Anchors:
- Commercial | Explicitly part of commercial segment expansion strategy to broaden the customer base
### Parent Offering:
- Palantir Foundry | Provides limited access to Foundry capabilities for development and testing
- Palantir AIP | Provides limited access to AIP capabilities for AI development and experimentation
### Child Offerings:
- none | No further decomposed tiers described
### Channels:
- Online | Self-service digital access through the company's online platforms and website
### Revenue Models:
- none | Revenue model not explicitly described; positioned as access expansion for exploration
