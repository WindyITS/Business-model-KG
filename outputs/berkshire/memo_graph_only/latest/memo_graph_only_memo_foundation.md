# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Berkshire Hathaway operates as a decentralized holding company generating revenue through five major business model anchors: (1) insurance underwriting and investment float generation, (2) freight rail transportation, (3) regulated utility and energy infrastructure, (4) diversified manufacturing of industrial, building and consumer products, and (5) service and retailing operations including aviation services, distribution, and automotive/home furnishings retail. The company emphasizes extreme decentralization in operations with centralized capital allocation, maintaining exceptional financial strength in insurance operations to generate "float" for investment while operating capital-intensive regulated utilities and rail franchises alongside cyclical manufacturing and consumer businesses.

## Explicit Support:
- "Berkshire Hathaway Inc. (“Berkshire,” “Company” or “Registrant”) is a holding company owning subsidiaries engaged in numerous diverse business activities."
- "The most important of these are insurance businesses, conducted on both a primary basis and a reinsurance basis, a freight rail transportation business and a group of utility and energy generation and distribution businesses."
- "Berkshire’s operating subsidiaries are managed on an unusually decentralized basis. There are few centralized or integrated business functions."
- "Float represents the approximate net policyholder funds generated through underwriting activities that are held for investment."

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Insurance
### Role in Business Model:
Underwrites property/casualty and life/health risks on both a primary and reinsurance basis worldwide. Generates underwriting profit and substantial investment "float" (policyholder funds held for investment), serving as the core capital allocation engine for the holding company structure.

### Explicit Support:
- "Berkshire’s insurance subsidiaries provide insurance and reinsurance of property and casualty risks as well as life and health risks worldwide."
- "Berkshire expects to achieve an underwriting profit over time."
- "Float represents the approximate net policyholder funds generated through underwriting activities that are held for investment."
- "Berkshire’s insurance companies maintain capital strength at exceptionally high levels, which differentiates them from their competitors."

### Customer Types:
- consumers | GEICO sells private passenger automobile insurance directly to individuals in all 50 states; also sells motorcycle, RV and boat insurance to individual consumers
- small businesses | BH Direct and GUARD target small and medium-sized businesses with commercial insurance products; surplus lines brokers serve smaller commercial accounts
- large enterprises | BHSI serves commercial and specialty insurance markets; reinsurance clients include domestic and foreign insurers and reinsurers worldwide
- government agencies | General Re Group serves public entity customers; municipal and government utility clients in energy sector
- healthcare organizations | MedPro Group provides healthcare liability insurance to physicians, surgeons, dentists, hospitals and healthcare facilities

### Channels:
- direct sales | GEICO utilizes direct response methods via Internet and telephone; BH Direct operates through internet-based platforms biBERK.com and Threeinsurance.com
- online | GEICO accepts applications via Internet; digital self-service platforms for insurance quoting and binding
- distributors | Wholesale and retail insurance agents and brokers; managing general agents used by BH Primary and BHRG operations
- resellers | Managing general agents and surplus lines brokers placing non-admitted insurance
