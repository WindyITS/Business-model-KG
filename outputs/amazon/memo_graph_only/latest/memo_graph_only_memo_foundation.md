# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Amazon operates through three distinct business segments: North America and International (geographic retail ecosystems encompassing online/physical marketplaces, device manufacturing, and service platforms for sellers, advertisers, and content creators) and Amazon Web Services (AWS) (a global cloud infrastructure business serving developers and enterprises). The company monetizes through a diversified portfolio including retail transactions, subscription memberships (Prime), consumption-based cloud services, seller service fees, advertising, and hardware sales. The business model centers on "customer obsession" achieved through low prices, vast selection, and convenience, serving multiple constituencies—consumers, sellers, developers, enterprises, content creators, and advertisers—via integrated online, physical, and cloud channels.

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] North America
### Role in Business Model:
Geographic segment housing the integrated retail marketplace, device manufacturing, digital content distribution, and service platforms (seller services, advertising, content creation programs) within North America. Serves as the operational and innovation hub for consumer-facing offerings and third-party seller infrastructure.
### Explicit Support:
- "We have organized our operations into three segments: North America, International, and Amazon Web Services ('AWS')."
- "We serve consumers through our online and physical stores..."
