# ANALYTICAL FRAME
## Summary:
Adobe Inc. operates a global software business centered on creativity, productivity, and customer experience management. The company organizes its operations into three reportable segments—Digital Media, Digital Experience, and Publishing and Advertising—serving business professionals, consumers, creative professionals, and marketing professionals. The business model is predominantly subscription-based, with revenue generated through cloud-hosted software-as-a-service (SaaS) offerings, term subscriptions, and managed services. Adobe's strategy focuses on integrating generative AI (Firefly) across its platforms, offering tiered subscription plans to cater to individual consumers, small businesses, and large enterprises, while maintaining direct sales forces for enterprise customers and digital channels for broader markets.

## Explicit Support:
- "In fiscal 2025, our business consisted of three reportable segments: Digital Media, Digital Experience and Publishing and Advertising."
- "We license our software-as-a-service solutions to customers over multi-year service terms and license the majority of our software products through a subscription model..."
- "We market our solutions directly to enterprise customers through our sales force... and directly to businesses and consumers."
- "Our focus revolves around serving our customer audiences: business professionals, consumers, creators, creative professionals and marketing professionals."

# SEGMENTS
## [Segment] Digital Media
### Role in Business Model:
Digital Media serves the productivity and creativity needs of business professionals, consumers, creators, and creative professionals. This segment generates revenue primarily through subscription offerings for document productivity (Acrobat), content creation (Creative Cloud), and AI-powered creative tools (Firefly). It represents Adobe's foundational creative and productivity franchise.

### Explicit Support:
- "Digital Media. To serve the productivity and creativity needs of business professionals, consumers, creators and creative professionals, our Digital Media solutions include our Adobe Acrobat solutions, Adobe Express and our Creative Cloud subscriptions and flagship apps... as well as Adobe Firefly."

### Customer Types:
- consumers | Served through freemium models (Acrobat Reader, limited Express) and individual subscriptions.
- small businesses | Served through Acrobat Standard and Pro plans, and Creative Cloud individual subscriptions.
- large enterprises | Served through Creative Cloud Pro and enterprise Acrobat deployments.

### Channels:
- direct sales | Used for enterprise customer engagements and large creative deployments.
- online | Primary channel for individual subscriptions through adobe.com and app stores.
- marketplaces | App stores for mobile and desktop software distribution.

## [Segment] Digital Experience
### Role in Business Model:
Digital Experience serves marketing professionals and large enterprises with end-to-end content supply chain solutions and customer experience orchestration. This segment provides enterprise-grade platforms for data management, content management, marketing automation, and commerce, monetized primarily through subscription and service fees. It integrates with the creative tools from Digital Media to form a unified content workflow.

### Explicit Support:
- "Digital Experience. To serve the end-to-end content supply chain needs of marketing professionals, our Digital Experience solutions include Adobe Experience Platform and native applications..."

### Customer Types:
- large enterprises | Primary target for Experience Platform, GenStudio, and enterprise marketing solutions.
- mid-market companies | Served through scaled versions of marketing automation and analytics tools.

### Channels:
- direct sales | Primary channel for enterprise customers through dedicated sales force and field offices.
- system integrators | Partners who implement and integrate Adobe solutions for enterprise customers.
- managed service providers | Partners who operate Adobe platforms for customers.

## [Segment] Publishing and Advertising
### Role in Business Model:
Publishing and Advertising contains legacy solutions including eLearning, technical document publishing, web conferencing, and Adobe Advertising. This segment represents mature, specialized offerings that are distinct from the core Digital Media and Digital Experience growth engines, with revenue from advertising, licensing, and service fees.

### Explicit Support:
- "Publishing and Advertising. Our Publishing and Advertising offerings contain legacy solutions including eLearning solutions, technical document publishing, web conferencing, document and forms platform, web App development, high-end printing through Adobe PostScript and Adobe PDF standards and our Adobe Advertising offerings."

### Customer Types:
- large enterprises | For enterprise advertising, technical publishing, and legacy platform solutions.

### Channels:
- direct sales | For enterprise advertising and publishing contracts.
- OEMs | For PostScript and PDF standards licensing to hardware manufacturers.
