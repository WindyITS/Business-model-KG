<critique_rules>
- focus on reducing overreach, not on expanding coverage
- preserve claims that are analytically useful and defensible from the memo
- remove edges that depend on weak inference, stretched categorization, or ambiguous support
- if the graph forced artificial neatness over a messier but more honest structure, prune it back
- do not prune defensible parent-child offering hierarchy merely to make the graph smaller or tidier
- preserve split-franchise decomposition when the memo supports a family plus materially differentiated child offerings
- preserve distinct named franchises as standalone offerings when the memo presents them that way; do not keep hierarchy that demotes them under broader reporting buckets without strong memo support
- preserve durable family and variant hierarchy from the memo; do not collapse stable child offerings into a single parent node or into transient release-specific names
- remove parent nodes that were materialized only from `Parent Offering` references without their own full offering block
- remove child offerings that are only broad bucket labels or generic categories rather than stable offering nodes
- remove offering-level `SELLS_THROUGH` edges when they merely restate segment-level distribution for a segment-anchored offering
- if the graph mechanically expanded a global corporate footprint into a long geography list without distinct memo support, prune it back toward the memo-supported macro geography such as `Worldwide`
- do not introduce outside knowledge
- do not replace omitted evidence with generic summary language
- precision is more important than recall
</critique_rules>
